Executive Summary:
During Q3 FY2025, the company recorded a 12% year-on-year increase in revenue driven by stronger investment banking fees and robust trading performance in the derivatives segment. However, expenses rose by 8% due to technology modernization and regulatory compliance initiatives.

Business Highlights:
- Equity trading volumes increased by 15%.
- Debt underwriting revenue rose 9%, led by corporate issuances in energy and healthcare.
- Wealth management continued steady growth, with AUM up 7%.

Risk & Compliance:
The major risks identified this quarter include:
1. Market Volatility: The derivatives desk remains exposed to fluctuations in global interest rates and commodity prices.
2. Credit Risk: Slight increase in counterparty risk exposure in the corporate lending portfolio.
3. Operational Risk: System migration to cloud infrastructure caused intermittent downtime in two regional data centers.
4. Regulatory Risk: Additional compliance reviews initiated by the Monetary Authority for anti-money laundering (AML) practices.

- Total revenue: $6.8 billion
- Net profit: $1.2 billion
- Cost-to-income ratio: 63%
- CET1 Capital Ratio: 14.1%

Outlook:
Management expects continued momentum in investment banking with moderate risk appetite. Focus areas include expansion of AI-driven trading analytics and enhanced data governance.

Conclusion:
While profitability remains strong, the firm must continue to invest in cybersecurity and risk management to maintain resilience under volatile market conditions.
